## Step 1 — Self-projection (PASS)

`ref_selfproj.dat` via `00b_self_projection_renumbered.sh`:

- `s` monotonic across all 15 frames (1.091 → 14.909).
- Middle frames essentially at integer indices (2.0001, 3.0000, ..., 13.0000).
- Endpoints `1.091318 / 14.908700` — matches the analytic boundary-bias prediction `(1 + 0.1·2)/(1 + 0.1) ≈ 1.09` / `14.91` under `exp(-λ·MSD_neighbor) = 0.10` (with `λ = 379.77 nm⁻²`).
- `z` ≈ `−0.00025` everywhere (floating-point noise, effectively zero).

Conclusion: **path reference file, atom ordering, MODEL ordering, PBC handling, and λ convention are all correct.** No technical bug at the path-construction layer.

(Driver-only atom-serial renumbering was required — see FP-030. Production GROMACS runs unaffected.)

`λ = 379.77 nm⁻²` is FP-022-settled (per-atom MSD vs total-SD convention; SI's "80" is total SD Å²). This VERDICT does **not** revisit λ. FP-027 documents a prior attempt to re-open it as a wasted 3 h of diagnosis.

## Step 2 — PATHMSD vs FUNCPATHMSD equivalence (PASS)

- `max |abs z-diff| = 0.000e+00` (gate: `< 1e-2`)

No λ-convention mismatch between the two; no atom-mapping discrepancy. Rules out the FP-020 class of bug (per-atom MSD vs total-MSD λ divergence).

## Step 3 — O-endpoint local tangent alignment (SIGNAL, not a verdict)

`o_endpoint_tangent_probe_P1.out` via `02_o_endpoint_tangent.py`, trajectory = first 2 ns of probe_P1 MetaD (stride 10, 21 snapshots).

| metric | value |
|---|---|
| ‖t_O‖ (reference O-end chord magnitude) | 16.471 Å |
| ‖Δx_t‖ range | [7.85, 23.96] Å, **mean = 16.27 Å** |
| cos(θ) range | [−0.206, −0.001], **mean = −0.111** |
| f_perp range | [0.979, 1.000], **mean = 0.992** |
| frames flagged OFF-TANGENT | **21 / 21 (100 %)** |

Raw reading:

- Walker motion magnitude `‖Δx_t‖ ≈ 16 Å` is comparable to the chord length itself — the protein is genuinely moving on the 112-Cα subspace, not frozen.
- cos(θ) sits within ±0.2 of zero — the motion direction has essentially no projection onto the linear O-end tangent. 99 % of the displacement lives in directions orthogonal to what the path CV projects onto `s`.

### Why this is a signal, not a root-cause verdict

A single 2 ns stalled probe with OFF-TANGENT motion is consistent with **at least two distinct causes**, and this suite cannot separate them:

**Hypothesis A — chord geometry weak at O-end.** The linear Cartesian chord between 1WDW and 3CEP has `∇_Cart s` nearly orthogonal to the directions in which the walker actually moves at the O endpoint, so even a well-sized bias cannot convert thermal motion into `s` progression.

**Hypothesis B — filling-pattern σ too narrow.** Our `plumed.dat` uses `ADAPTIVE=GEOM SIGMA=0.1 SIGMA_MIN=0.3,0.005 SIGMA_MAX=1.0,0.05`. Every SIGMA value is ⚠️ **UNVERIFIED (SI silent)** per `replication/parameters/JACS2019_MetaDynamics_Parameters.md §3`; the SI only says "adaptive Gaussian width scheme". If the GEOM-projected σ at s=1 produces a bias force too small to compete with the local FES restoring force, the walker does mostly thermal drift — which, in a 336-D Cα space, projects ~orthogonal to any 1-D tangent by default. That alone would reproduce the 21/21 OFF-TANGENT signature without requiring chord weakness.

FP-029 explicitly warns against treating adaptive-mode failures as geometry verdicts without primary-source separation. This section honors that warning.

### What step 3 does tell us

- Path CV machinery is clean: steps 1+2 rule out PATHMSD / λ / metric bugs.
- The stall is in one (or both) of: chord geometry at O-end, filling-pattern σ configuration.
- We cannot declare a root cause from this data alone.
