ABS Abstract
ABS

We present STAR-MD (Scalable Spatio-Temporal SE(3) Diffusion for Long-Horizon Protein Dynamics), a generative model designed to produce physically realistic all-atom protein trajectories at timescales previously inaccessible to most MD-based generative approaches. Existing generative methods either fail catastrophically when extrapolated beyond their training horizon or suffer from O(N³L) memory scaling that makes them impractical for longer sequences or trajectories. STAR-MD addresses both limitations through two innovations: (1) a SE(3)-equivariant diffusion process that separately handles translational (standard Gaussian) and rotational (IGSO₃) degrees of freedom, preserving physical symmetry throughout generation; and (2) a Causal Diffusion Transformer with joint spatio-temporal attention that achieves O(N²L²) memory complexity — delivering a 196× reduction in KV-cache memory vs. ConfRover at N=200, L=32. On the ATLAS benchmark (100 ns), STAR-MD achieves 0.54 conformational recall and 86.81% combined structural validity. Critically, on long-horizon extrapolation to 1 μs, recall improves to 0.61, with validity maintained at 79.93% — whereas all prior models degrade catastrophically at this scale.

§1 问题背景 — The Timescale Challenge
P1

Molecular dynamics simulation is the gold standard for studying protein conformational dynamics, but it faces a fundamental computational barrier: the timescales relevant to most biologically important processes — protein folding, allosteric conformational changes, ligand binding, and catalytic cycle transitions — span microseconds to milliseconds, while current MD can economically access only nanoseconds to microseconds per simulation. This "timescale gap" is not a mere engineering problem solvable by faster hardware; it reflects a fundamental gap between the characteristic relaxation times of biological processes and the step size of atomistic integrators (typically 2 fs). For a protein like TrpB, the COMM domain O→C conformational transition occurs on the microsecond-to-millisecond timescale. Even with GPU-accelerated MD, 500 ns of unbiased simulation is insufficient to sample a single O→C transition — a fact directly demonstrated in JACS 2019 (Osuna lab). This motivates enhanced sampling approaches, of which two categories have emerged: physics-based (MetaDynamics, OPES, replica exchange) and machine learning-based (generative trajectory models).

P2

Prior generative models for protein dynamics have attempted to bridge this gap with varying degrees of success. FrameDiff demonstrated SE(3)-equivariant diffusion for protein structure generation but was designed for static structures, not dynamic trajectories. MDGen applied diffusion to MD trajectory generation but suffered from poor structural validity at long timescales. ConfRover — perhaps the closest precursor to STAR-MD — achieved competitive performance at 100 ns but used a Pairformer-based architecture with O(N³L + N²L²) memory complexity, making it computationally prohibitive for proteins with N > 300 residues or trajectories with L > 64 frames. Its most critical failure: ConfRover fails catastrophically when rolled out beyond its training horizon (~100 ns), generating structurally invalid trajectories at 240 ns and 1 μs. AlphaFold, while capable of generating diverse protein conformations, achieves near-zero combined structural validity (0.47%) when evaluated as a trajectory generator — it can produce multiple static structures, but these are not dynamically connected trajectories and lack temporal coherence.

§2 方法架构 — SE(3) Diffusion + Causal Transformer
P3

The foundation of STAR-MD is a SE(3)-equivariant diffusion process that separately models translations and rotations. For a protein with N residues, the state at each frame t is represented as a set of N rigid body frames {T₁, ..., T_N}, where each Tᵢ ∈ SE(3) encodes both the position and orientation of residue i. The forward diffusion process adds noise independently to the translational and rotational components:

  • Translations: standard Gaussian diffusion — x_τ = √(ᾱ_τ)x₀ + √(1−ᾱ_τ)ε, ε ~ N(0, I). This is identical to DDPM-style diffusion in Euclidean space.
  • Rotations: IGSO₃ diffusion — R_τ = Exp(ω_τ)R₀, where ω_τ is sampled from an isotropic Gaussian on the tangent space of SO(3). This ensures that rotational noise respects the non-Euclidean geometry of the rotation group.

The key physical requirement is that the generative model must be equivariant: rotating the entire protein in 3D space should produce a trajectory that is simply the rotated version of the original. By building equivariance directly into the diffusion noise process (rather than imposing it as a post-processing constraint), STAR-MD guarantees this property for any generated trajectory.

为什么旋转不能用普通高斯噪声？SO(3) 不是欧氏空间——你不能简单地对旋转矩阵加高斯噪声（结果不再是旋转矩阵）。IGSO₃ 在 SO(3) 的黎曼流形上定义噪声，保证每一步扩散后仍然得到合法的旋转。这是 SE(3) 扩散模型的核心数学挑战。
与你的项目：你的 MetaDynamics 使用笛卡尔坐标 + FUNCPATHMSD，不需要显式处理旋转等变性（PLUMED 在 MSD 计算中自动对齐结构）。STAR-MD 的 SE(3) 框架更接近蛋白质物理的本质表示，但计算上也更复杂。
P4

The denoising network in STAR-MD is a Causal Diffusion Transformer (Causal DiT) that jointly models spatial interactions between residues and temporal dependencies between frames. Given a noisy trajectory {x̃_{1:L}}, the network predicts the denoised trajectory through two types of attention:

  • Spatial attention: Standard multi-head self-attention over N residues at each frame. Complexity per frame: O(N²). This captures residue–residue interactions within each snapshot.
  • Temporal attention with causal masking: Each residue attends to its own representations from all previous frames, using a lower-triangular attention mask. Complexity: O(L²) per residue, or O(N·L²) total. This captures temporal correlations in protein dynamics.

Combined complexity: O(N²L²). Compared to ConfRover's Pairformer (O(N³L + N²L²)), STAR-MD eliminates the cubic N³ term — the dominant cost for large proteins. At N=200, L=32, ConfRover requires ~1.3 GB per attention layer while STAR-MD requires ~6.6 MB, a 196× reduction. The causal masking also enables autoregressive generation: once trained, the network can generate trajectories of arbitrary length by rolling out frame-by-frame, each time conditioning on all previously generated frames.

与你的项目：如果将来你想在 TrpB 上运行 STAR-MD，需要确保 N（序列长度）≤ 384（训练时的截断限制），并且从 5DVZ 的 Open-state 起始结构开始。Causal DiT 会自回归生成后续帧，不需要你提供中间结构。
P5

STAR-MD generates long trajectories via autoregressive rollout with contextual noise injection. At inference time, the model generates trajectories in blocks of L frames, where each block is conditioned on the previous block (or the initial structure for the first block). The process is:

  1. Start from a known protein structure (e.g., a PDB crystal structure or AlphaFold model).
  2. Generate the first L frames by running the full diffusion reverse process (T denoising steps), conditioned on the starting structure.
  3. Generate the next L frames, conditioning on all previously generated frames using the causal attention mechanism.
  4. Repeat indefinitely to produce trajectories of arbitrary length.

A critical training detail is contextual noise injection: during training, diffusion noise τ ~ U[0, 0.1] is applied to the historical context (previously generated frames). This prevents the model from overfitting to exact atomic positions in the conditioning window and forces it to learn the distribution of trajectories rather than memorizing specific atomic trajectories. Without this regularization, autoregressive rollout accumulates errors rapidly and the trajectory diverges.

术语解释

噪声注入的直觉：如果训练时总给模型看"完美的"历史帧，推理时遇到略有误差的已生成帧就会失败（分布偏移）。加上小噪声让模型学会在"不完美条件"下工作——类似于 Dropout 的作用，但在数据层面而非激活层面。
局限：长时程时误差仍然累积。虽然上下文噪声注入延缓了误差累积，但 79.93% 的 1μs 有效性（vs 86.81% at 100ns）说明即使 STAR-MD，在更长时间尺度下结构有效性也会下降。这是所有自回归模型的固有限制。
与你的项目：STAR-MD 的生成轨迹在 1μs 仍有 20% 的"无效帧"。在对比实验中，你需要先过滤 STAR-MD 的无效构象（clash 检查、键长检查），然后再投影到你的路径 CV 做比较，否则无效构象会污染分析结果。
P6

STAR-MD is trained exclusively on the ATLAS dataset, a comprehensive collection of all-atom MD trajectories assembled specifically for benchmarking generative protein dynamics models. ATLAS contains 1,390 proteins, each simulated for 100 ns using a consistent MD protocol (CHARMM36m force field, TIP3P water, 300 K). Key dataset statistics:

  • Training split: 1,080 proteins, filtered to exclude sequences longer than 384 residues (hardware memory constraint).
  • Test split: 82 proteins, held out on a time-based split to prevent data leakage (newer proteins are in the test set).
  • Extended evaluation set: Custom 250 ns and 1 μs MD simulations run by the STAR-MD authors for long-horizon evaluation.

Importantly, ATLAS covers predominantly globular, soluble, well-characterized proteins — mostly from well-studied organisms at standard conditions (300 K). This has significant implications for out-of-distribution generalization: proteins that differ from this distribution (thermophilic enzymes, membrane proteins, multimeric complexes, disordered proteins) are likely to be out-of-distribution for STAR-MD.

段落功能：展示本文最重要的实验结果——长时程外推时 recall 提升（0.54→0.61）以及 ConfRover 的有效性崩塌。这是 STAR-MD 的决定性优势。

核心实验结果
recall 随时间增加的直觉：类比于用更多步骤的随机游走探索更大的空间——更长的生成轨迹允许 STAR-MD 访问更多的构象空间，接近参考 MD 构象集合的覆盖率上限。这说明 STAR-MD 的生成过程在时间维度上是"正确的随机游走"，而不是在固定空间内漫游。
ConfRover-W 在 1μs 的崩塌：有效性从 52% 降到 37%，但 recall 从 0.36 升到 0.45——这说明 ConfRover-W 在生成越来越多无效构象的同时，碰巧访问了更多参考 MD 构象。"高 recall + 低 validity" 意味着生成轨迹中混杂大量无效构象，不能直接用于科学分析。
对 Amin benchmark 的启示：如果 STAR-MD 在 TrpB 上运行 1μs，recall 可能接近 0.61（如果 TrpB 类似 ATLAS 分布中的蛋白质）。关键问题：TrpB 的 O→C 转变是否在 STAR-MD 的 1μs 生成中自发出现？这正是 benchmark 要测试的。
P9

The paper includes several ablation experiments that validate individual design choices. Key findings from these ablation studies:

  • SE(3) equivariance is essential: A version without SE(3) equivariance shows substantially degraded structural validity, confirming that respecting rotational/translational symmetry is not a cosmetic constraint but a functional necessity for physically valid generation.
  • Causal masking enables extrapolation: Without causal masking (i.e., non-autoregressive generation), the model cannot extrapolate beyond its training horizon. This directly confirms that the temporal causality is what enables long-horizon generation.
  • Contextual noise injection improves extrapolation validity: Removing contextual noise during training causes validity to degrade faster during autoregressive rollout — from 86.81% at 100 ns to below 60% at 1 μs vs. STAR-MD's 79.93%. The regularization effect is most important at long timescales.
  • IGSO₃ vs. Euclidean rotation noise: Using standard Gaussian noise for rotations (ignoring SO(3) geometry) leads to ~10% lower structural validity, confirming that proper geometric treatment of rotations matters.

§4 局限性 — Known Boundaries
P10

STAR-MD, despite its significant advances, has several fundamental limitations that must be understood before applying it to any specific scientific problem:

  1. No free energy output: STAR-MD generates kinetically plausible trajectories but cannot output free energy values (ΔG, ΔF). Conformational recall measures which states are visited, not their thermodynamic probability. Without free energy information, you cannot determine whether the O state is more stable than the C state, or compute the activation barrier for the conformational transition.
  2. Training distribution bias: ATLAS covers ~1,390 globular soluble proteins, predominantly from mesophilic organisms at 300 K. Membrane proteins, intrinsically disordered regions, multimeric enzymes, and thermophilic proteins (like TrpB from P. furiosus, optimal at 100°C) are likely out-of-distribution. The model inherits the CHARMM36m force field biases of ATLAS simulations.
  3. No explicit rare event biasing: Unlike MetaDynamics (which adds a bias potential to push the system over free energy barriers), STAR-MD explores stochastically. There is no guarantee that a rare conformational event (e.g., the O→C transition for TrpB) will be sampled even in a 1 μs generation, if the true barrier crossing probability is very low.
  4. Sequence length limit: Training excluded proteins with N > 384 residues. Performance on longer proteins is untested and potentially poor.

  1. 运行 STAR-MD 推理，从 5DVZ（TrpB O-state）起始，生成 1μs 等效轨迹
