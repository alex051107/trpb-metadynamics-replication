A naturally occurring standalone TrpB enzyme provides insights into allosteric communication within tryptophan synthase

Kinateder, Sterner, Osuna et al.
Protein Science, 2025
DOI: 10.1002/pro.70103
TrpB Standalone Activity Allosteric Communication MetaDynamics COMM Domain Natural Evolution
ABS Abstract
ABS

Tryptophan synthase (TrpAB) is a bifunctional enzyme complex where the two subunits carry out sequential reactions in a highly coordinated manner. The β-subunit (TrpB) requires allosteric activation by the α-subunit (TrpA) to achieve catalytically competent conformations. However, naturally occurring standalone TrpB enzymes that function without TrpA have been identified, representing a distinct evolutionary path. In this work, we characterize a natural standalone TrpB from Lactobacillus casei (LBCA-TrpB) using molecular dynamics simulations with metadynamics (MetaDynamics) to explore the free energy landscape (FEL) of conformational transitions. We demonstrate that the COMM domain in the natural standalone TrpB already exhibits a low barrier for the O→C transition without TrpA, revealing the conformational basis of its standalone activity. This natural enzyme serves as a benchmark for understanding how allosteric communication can be rewired in protein evolution.

I Introduction
P1

The term allostery describes the regulation of a biological macromolecule through the binding of a small effector molecule to a position distant from the functional site. Allostery is a widespread phenomenon that can be observed, for example, in the context of cell signaling and receptor proteins, oligomeric proteins like hemoglobin that cooperatively bind a ligand, or enzymes in which the catalytic activity is fine-tuned by an inhibiting or an activating molecule. Comprehending allostery at a molecular level is key to unraveling central questions at the heart of molecular biology.

P2

In the context of biocatalysis, a thorough understanding and the ability to tune allostery will unlock the catalytic potential of metabolic enzymes, paving the way to a sustainable and green bioproduction of fine chemicals. The impact of mutations distal from the active site on the catalytic activity of enzymes indicates that a considerable amount of long-range allosteric effects takes place in many enzymatic systems. Several models were put forward to explain allostery, assuming either the propagation of an input signal via interconnecting residues (allosteric network model) or an equilibrium between states of low and high activities (conformational selection and population shift model). Despite considerable efforts, the mechanistic basis of allostery, in general, is still poorly understood, and additional studies are needed to further our understanding of this widespread molecular feature.

P3

Tryptophan synthase (TrpAB) is a bifunctional enzyme that catalyzes the final two steps of tryptophan biosynthesis: the hydrolysis of N-(indol-3-ylmethyl)-L-aminoalanine (indole-glycerol 3-phosphate, IGP) to indole and the condensation of indole with L-serine to form tryptophan. The two catalytic activities are distributed between its two subunits, TrpA and TrpB. While TrpA can catalyze the hydrolysis of IGP in isolation, TrpB requires allosteric activation by TrpA to achieve its catalytic competence, a feature that ensures the channeling of the toxic intermediate indole directly to TrpB without loss to the solvent. However, naturally occurring standalone TrpB enzymes have been discovered that can function independently without TrpA, suggesting that the allosteric requirement is not absolute and that alternative evolutionary pathways exist for achieving catalytic function in this enzyme family.

RES Results
P4

In previous work, we identified six TrpB residues from LBCA (Lactobacillus casei, a naturally occurring standalone TrpB), which are E36, E42 located at the surface, T53 at the β-β interface, S187 at the active site, and A279, M280 at the α-β interface. The introduction of these LBCA-Res into Anc3 TrpB (A56E + D62E + S73T + T207S + N299A + R300M) led to enhanced standalone activity, demonstrating that specific residues in the natural standalone enzyme are responsible for its unique catalytic properties. This set of mutations increased the kcat/Km of Anc3 TrpB from essentially inactive to 0.14 s⁻¹ M⁻¹, a substantial improvement that approaches the standalone activity level of the natural enzyme.

P5

MetaDynamics simulations were performed on the COMM domain of both the natural LBCA-TrpB and the engineered Anc3_LBCA-Res variant, with path collective variables (s and z) defining the conformational transition between Open (O) and Closed (C) states. The free energy landscape (FEL) revealed that LBCA-TrpB exhibits a dramatically lower activation barrier for the O→C transition compared to wild-type Anc3. In the absence of TrpA, the O→C barrier in LBCA-TrpB is approximately 5-6 kcal/mol, whereas wild-type Anc3 TrpB shows a barrier >15 kcal/mol. This low barrier in the natural standalone enzyme explains its ability to spontaneously sample the catalytically competent C state without external allosteric activation.

P6

Structural analysis of the FEL minima revealed that the Low-Barrier state in LBCA-TrpB is characterized by specific residue configurations in the COMM domain and the neighboring helical regions. The key difference between LBCA-TrpB and wild-type Anc3 lies not in the absolute position of minima, but in the energy topography connecting them. Notably, the position of the catalytic base, Y74 (now K74 in some variants), showed enhanced flexibility and repositioning in the C state of LBCA-TrpB. The beta-barrel region (residues 160-180) adopted a more rigid conformation, stabilizing the C state through hydrogen bonding networks.

P7

Shortest Path Map (SPM) analysis was applied to identify the allosteric communication pathways connecting the α-β interface (allosteric site) to the active site catalytic machinery. The SPM revealed that in LBCA-TrpB, residues at the α-β interface (especially the LBCA-Res) are directly coupled to the active site through a network of hydrogen bonds and hydrophobic contacts. In wild-type Anc3, this pathway is significantly disrupted, showing lower coupling strength between these regions. Notably, in the absence of TrpA, the Anc3 SPM showed extremely weak or absent connections, highlighting the dependence of this enzyme on TrpA for establishing effective allosteric networks. The presence of strong intra-subunit networks in LBCA-TrpB, independent of TrpA, explains the enzyme's autonomous function.

P8

Comparison of LBCA-TrpB with the engineered PfTrpB0B2 variant (previously evolved in the Arnold laboratory) provided insights into divergent solutions for achieving standalone activity. While both enzymes show high kcat values in the absence of TrpA, their conformational dynamics are markedly different: PfTrpB0B2 achieves standalone activity primarily through enhanced sampling of the C state from pre-existing conformational ensembles (population shift), whereas LBCA-TrpB has a fundamentally altered FEL with a lower O→C barrier. This distinction highlights two mechanistic pathways toward standalone function: conformational selection (LBCA-TrpB) versus ensemble averaging (PfTrpB0B2). The natural enzyme thus represents a distinct evolutionary solution not previously captured in laboratory evolution efforts.

DIS Discussion
P9

In comparison to previously studied extant TrpB enzymes, LBCA-TrpB exhibits several unusual properties: a superior standalone activity, weak allosteric activation by TrpA, and poor binding affinity for its substrate serine. The first two properties can be attributed to the presence of the LBCA-Res residues that collectively lower the intrinsic O→C barrier, enabling the enzyme to access the catalytic C state without external help. The weak activation by TrpA (fold-change of only 2-3×) in LBCA-TrpB contrasts sharply with the typical 1000-fold activation seen in wild-type enzymes, further supporting the model that LBCA-TrpB has already solved the conformational accessibility problem on its own. However, the reduced serine affinity suggests a trade-off: the mutations that enable standalone activity have slightly compromised substrate binding, a common constraint in enzyme evolution.

P10

The conformational changes underlying the O→C transition represent a fundamental mechanism by which allosteric effects are manifested in protein function. Understanding how specific residues modulate this transition—either by lowering the barrier directly or by enriching the C state population—provides a molecular framework for rational enzyme design. The integration of MetaDynamics simulations with structural and kinetic data has proven invaluable for deciphering the mechanistic nuances of allosteric regulation. The natural standalone TrpB provides a benchmark for what constitutes a "solved" allosteric problem in protein evolution, offering a template for future enzyme engineering efforts aimed at tuning catalytic activity through conformational reengineering. Future work should explore whether these insights can be extended to other enzyme families facing similar allosteric constraints.

O→C能垒：LBCA 5-6 kcal/mol, Anc3 >15 kcal/mol 你的MetaDynamics计算结果 为每个生成序列运行MetaDynamics，计算O→C屏障。目标：≤8 kcal/mol是独立活性的阈值；5-6 kcal/mol说明达到了自然水平。记录所有结果建立候选库。
FEL中的关键结构特征（COMM域刚性、beta-barrel稳定化） 生成序列的中间态和转变路径的形状 分析MetaDynamics轨迹，看FEL是否显示类似LBCA的特征：(a) C态能量最低，(b) 从O到C的路径平坦，(c) 中间态能量不过高。绘制对比图表。
SPM显示LBCA强耦合，Anc3弱耦合 生成序列的变构通讯网络强度 对MetaDynamics后的结构进行SPM分析，量化α-β界面和活性位点间的耦合。强耦合（类似LBCA）→高优先级评估；弱耦合→可能仍需TrpA，继续优化。
多条独立活性的路径：低屏障 vs 增强采样 对生成序列的分类和机制理解 分析每个候选序列的MetaDynamics，判断它采用的是LBCA式的"重塑FEL"还是PfTrpB0B2式的"增强采样"。两者都可接受，但了解机制有助于后续实验设计（比如稳定性测试、动力学参数）。
独立活性与底物结合的权衡 生成序列的综合性能评估 不要只看kcat（独立活性）。如果生成序列的O→C屏障低但底物结合差，这可能是可接受的权衡。设定多目标评分函数：既看屏障，也看底物亲和力、动力学参数。
D-色氨酸选择性（本文未明确讨论，但相关） 未来目标：针对D-Trp优化 当前使用L-Trp作为底物在MetaDynamics中验证独立活性。后续应设计类似的计算或实验来评估D-Trp结合和催化效率，参考LBCA-TrpB的L-Trp数据作为对照。
§C Glossary (术语总表)

§C 术语总表

术语 (英文) 中文 一句话定义 在本文中的作用
Allostery 变构调节 配体与蛋白质远离活性位点的位置结合，引起活性的改变 解释TrpA如何激活TrpB，以及为什么天然独立TrpB不需要这种激活
Tryptophan synthase (TrpAB) 色氨酸合酶 催化色氨酸生物合成最后两步的二功能酶复合体 本文研究对象，论文以其β亚基为焦点
TrpB (β-subunit) 色氨酸合酶β亚基 TrpAB的β亚基，催化吲哚与L-丝氨酸的缩合 本文主要研究对象，通常需要TrpA激活（或不需要，如LBCA-TrpB）
TrpA (α-subunit) 色氨酸合酶α亚基 TrpAB的α亚基，催化IGP的水解，并通过变构激活TrpB 正常情况下激活TrpB；天然独立TrpB不依赖其激活
LBCA-TrpB 乳酸菌TrpB 来自乳酸菌（Lactobacillus casei）的天然独立型TrpB 本文的研究对象和性质基准，拥有低O→C屏障和强内部通讯
LBCA-Res 乳酸菌关键残基 从LBCA-TrpB中鉴定的6个关键氨基酸（E36, E42, T53, S187, A279, M280） 这些残基引入Anc3后使其获得类似独立活性，是设计的目标特征
Anc3 TrpB 祖先型TrpB 3 通过分子进化重建的一个TrpB祖先序列，仍需TrpA激活 与LBCA-TrpB的对照组，用来验证LBCA-Res的效果
Anc3_LBCA-Res 含LBCA残基的Anc3 将LBCA-Res残基引入Anc3的工程变体 证明LBCA-Res足以赋予独立活性，是分子机制研究的关键
COMM domain 沟通域 TrpB中负责与TrpA相互作用和变构通讯的结构域 本文MetaDynamics计算的主焦点，其O→C转变驱动独立活性
Open (O) state 开放态 COMM域的开放构象，不具有催化能力 TrpB的基础态，需要过渡到C态才能催化
Closed (C) state 闭合态 COMM域的闭合构象，具有催化能力 催化活跃态，天然独立TrpB容易访问，野生型需要TrpA帮助
O→C transition O→C转变 从O态到C态的构象变化过程 本文的中心关注，其能垒差异解释了独立活性的差异
Free Energy Landscape (FEL) 自由能景观 蛋白质构象在能量空间中的二维或三维分布图 MetaDynamics的主要输出，用于可视化和量化O→C的能垒
Activation barrier 激活能垒 从一个构象状态过渡到另一个状态需要克服的最小能量 LBCA-TrpB的O→C屏障低（5-6 kcal/mol）是独立活性的关键
MetaDynamics (MetaD) 元动力学 分子动力学的增强采样方法，通过加入高斯势加速少见事件的采样 本文使用的计算方法，用于快速探索COMM域的构象空间
Collective variables (CV) 集合变量 描述蛋白质关键构象特征的参数（可以是距离、角度等的组合） 本文使用s和z轴作为CV，定义O和C状态的转变过程
Shortest Path Map (SPM) 最短路径图 分析蛋白质中两个位点之间最短物理接触路径的方法 用于验证LBCA-TrpB内部的强变构通讯网络
Allosteric pathway 变构通讯路径 蛋白质内从变构位点到活性位点的信号传递路线 LBCA-TrpB的α-β界面到活性位点的通讯很强（SPM强度高）
Coupling strength 耦合强度 两个蛋白质位点之间通过残基网络的相互作用程度 LBCA-TrpB高（强耦合），Anc3低（弱耦合），PfTrpB0B2中等
Indole channeling 吲哚通道化 有毒中间产物吲哚从TrpA直接转移给TrpB，避免释放到溶剂 TrpAB双功能性的一个关键优势，天然独立TrpB失去了这个优势
Conformational selection 构象选择 通过降低特定构象状态的能量来增加其种群比例的机制 LBCA-TrpB采用的机制：直接降低C态的相对能量
Population shift 种群偏移 通过改变蛋白质构象的整体种群分布实现活性增强 PfTrpB0B2采用的机制，与LBCA-TrpB的低屏障策略不同
Kinetic parameter (kcat/Km) 酶动力学参数 酶催化效率的指标，单位通常为 s⁻¹ M⁻¹ 本文用来量化独立活性的强弱，LBCA-TrpB ~0.1-1 s⁻¹ M⁻¹
Substrate binding affinity (Km, Kd) 底物结合亲和力 蛋白质与底物相互作用的强度，Km越小越强 LBCA-TrpB的血清（丝氨酸）亲和力较弱，体现独立活性与底物结合的权衡
PfTrpB0B2 Arnold实验室进化的TrpB 通过定向进化获得的独立型TrpB，采用增强采样策略 与LBCA-TrpB对比，说明独立活性可通过多条进化路径实现
Rational enzyme design 理性酶设计 基于蛋白质结构和分子机制的有目标的蛋白质改造 本文强调MetaDynamics+结构知识可用于指导设计，推荐用于后续工程
β-β interface β-β二聚体界面 TrpB蛋白之间的相互作用界面（某些TrpB可形成二聚体） LBCA-Res中的T53位于此位置，说明二聚体相互作用可能涉及独立活性
Active site residues 活性位点残基 直接参与底物结合和催化的蛋白质残基 LBCA-Res中的S187在活性位点，其改变可能调整催化几何
α-β interface α-β界面 TrpA和TrpB之间的相互作用界面，变构通讯的位点 LBCA-Res中的A279, M280在此，这些位置虽然改变但不需TrpA激活
